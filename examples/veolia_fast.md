# GenAI Opportunity Report — Veolia

## Company Context

Veolia reported record financial results in 2025, with revenue of €44.4 billion and an EBITDA margin of 15.9%, exceeding guidance [source](https://www.investing.com/news/transcripts/earnings-call-transcript-veolia-environnement-reports-historic-high-in-q4-2025-93CH-4526984). The company completed major acquisitions, including the $3 billion acquisition of Clean Earth (US hazardous waste) and significant Water Technologies deals [source](https://www.reuters.com/business/frances-veolia-buy-hazardous-waste-group-clean-earth-3-billion-2025-11-21/). Veolia’s GreenUp strategic plan (2024–2027) drove these results, with €2.3 billion invested in Water Technologies and Hazardous Waste outside Europe [source](https://www.veolia.com/en/our-media/press-releases/full-year-2025-results).

Veolia operates in three main business lines: Water, Waste, and Energy. The company’s mission is to “resource the world” by helping customers address environmental and sustainability challenges in energy, water, and waste [source](https://defendpak.veoliaes.com/about_us). Veolia’s target markets include municipalities, energy and manufacturing firms, healthcare, and commercial real estate, with 80% of its revenue generated internationally [source](https://matrixbcg.com/blogs/target-market/veolia).

## 1. Data Center Resource 360 Multimodal Optimization

### The Opportunity

Optimizing the complex interactions between water, energy, and waste management in data centers is a multifaceted challenge. This requires Veolia's Data Center Resource 360 offering and its focus on creating carbon-neutral, water-positive, circular hubs. This use case leverages Veolia's unique Data Center Resource 360 offering and its focus on creating carbon-neutral, water-positive, circular hubs, which competitors cannot replicate due to the lack of an integrated approach and proprietary technologies.

### How The Workflow Would Work

1. **User input:** Data center sensor data, energy consumption data, water usage data, waste management logs.
2. **Retrieved or generated context:** Data Center Resource 360 specifications and historical operational data.
3. **Generated output:** Optimized operational strategies that balance water, energy, and waste management, including detailed resource allocation plans, efficiency recommendations, and sustainability impact assessments.
4. **Human approval or decision point:** Data center managers and sustainability consultants review and approve the generated strategies before implementation.

### Why GenAI Fits

GenAI adds value by synthesizing complex, multimodal data from data centers into actionable operational strategies that human experts can review and refine. Classical systems handle structured data and deterministic optimization well, but GenAI excels in interpreting unstructured sensor data and generating comprehensive, adaptive strategies.

### Data and Integration Needs

Data center sensor data, energy consumption data, water usage data, waste management logs, and Data Center Resource 360 specifications.

### Impact To Validate

- **Resource Utilization Efficiency** matters because it measures the efficiency gains in resource utilization achieved through GenAI-optimized operational strategies. Measure it by tracking the resource utilization metrics before and after implementing GenAI-generated strategies. Compare against current resource utilization efficiency metrics. The target direction is to increase.
- **Sustainability Impact Score** matters because it assesses the improvements in sustainability impact achieved through GenAI-optimized operational strategies. Measure it by measuring the sustainability impact metrics before and after implementing GenAI-generated strategies. Compare against current sustainability impact scores. The target direction is to increase.

### Risks and Mitigations

- **Data integration challenges:** Ensure robust data pipelines and integration protocols to handle multimodal data from various sensors and systems.
- **Model interpretability and trust:** Implement explainable AI techniques and human-in-the-loop validation to ensure transparency and trust in the generated strategies.
- **Operational disruption during implementation:** Phase the implementation and provide comprehensive training to minimize operational disruptions.
